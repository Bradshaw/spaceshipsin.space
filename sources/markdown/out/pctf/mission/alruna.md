## The Situation

Current date: 17th of October, 1998

Alruna is a medical transportation and waste management company that has been providing services for a larger corporation, Awen-Volos. Some of those services use paranormal methods to extract and manipulate information.
Medical waste contains memories. Alruna's incinerators extract them, and flush them into a labyrinthian complex of tunnels and pipes, called the "Runoff" that filters and condenses the memories into a holding pool.
Awen-Volos agents occasionally take samples from the holding pool before dumping a mysterious chemical into it and emptying it into the city's sewers.

It appears that Alruna is also capable of duplicating certain shipments, notably organ donations, so that a delivery can be made while the duplicate is incinerated. Sometimes people are duplicated and murdered so that their bodies can be incinerated to extract information, while leaving the original alive to avoid suspicion.

## Introduction

A report about the medical transportation and waste management company, Alruna, has been forwarded to the PCTF. The report was sent by Cleo Villegas, a low level employee.
She claims that there are many discrepancies in the records. For example, some shipments numbers have multiple transporter assignments, and others are labeled with names of people who are still alive.
She brought up the claims with her manager, Dylan Cobb, who she hasn't seen since. His replacement, Grace Hansen, is cold and distant towards her, along with her other colleagues. When she tried to bring up Dylan, her colleagues quickly changed the subject. She suspects foul play and has filed for sick leave, preferring to stay away from work.

The report contains two photographs of the body of Jordan Walters, a murder victim. Both photos show bullet wounds to the head and chest, and scars from liver and kidney removals for organ donation.

### Incinerator Hall

A row of incinerators line both sides of the hall.
The incinerators are connected to gas and oxygen pipes at the rear, and a water pipe to the top. Waste is deposited into a fold-out trapdoor. A firing cycle lasts about ten seconds, with fuel and oxygen being blasted into the chamber. After firing, water is pumped into the incinerator, flashing to steam and cleaning the chamber. Water continues to pump until the temperature is low enough that it no longer boils. The ashes and water flush into collection pipes under the incinerator and into the runoff system below.
