# Para-Criminality Task Force Setting and Lore

The Para-Criminality Task Force investigates, prevents, and prosecutes crimes that involve paranormal activity.

## The PCTF

Founded in 1953 as an offshoot of the Domestic Surveillance Agency.
It it still officially part of the DSA, but operates independently. Part of the PCTF's funding goes through the DSA, although this is to dissimulate the true finances and logistics, which are mostly provided via military R&D programmes.

The PCTF cooperates with its counterpart in the Bureau of Foreign Affairs: the Deep Reconnaissance Division. The PCTF and the DRD share general information about the Beyond and occasionally work together on cases that straddle each others' jurisdictions. The two organisations share some technology, but only rarely. Their needs and doctrines do not overlap much, so while early technologies (null-imprinted munitions, antimemetics, anticognitives, etc...) were essential to both, current needs have diverged significantly.

In public-facing documentation, PCTF stands for "Procurement Compliance Task Force".
The true nature of the PCTF is highly secret. Practically all of its employees and agents have had their previous lives completely erased, their former families and friends brainwashed with anticognitive drugs and seeded with new memories. Only a handful of people within the PCTF have unmodified memories.

When a new Chancellor is elected, they visit the PCTF headquarters and meet with the PCTF Council, upon which they are administered anticognitives. This allows the PCTF to take orders from and report back to the Chancellor with the knowledge of the true nature of the task force disappearing from conscious thought at all other times. Other politicians are occasionally brought in for consultation, but they typically undergo a complete wipe immediately afterwards.

### The Council

The council is constituted of eight to sixteen members. Councilmembers are picked from other government agencies or promoted from within the agency. Their terms are limited to a randomly chosen duration of three to five years, after which their knowledge is transferred back to the council and they receive a total brainwipe or are simply disposed of.
The founder of the PCTF, Erin Burke, created the first council in 1957. She transferred her memories to twelve hand-picked agents and took an extreme dose of anticognitives. While not fatal, she was unable to retain any memories for more than a few minutes until her death in 1993. This put an end to the "trust crisis". Her intentions for the task force were no longer in doubt. The council vowed to do the same when their own terms came to an end and the process has been enforced ever since.

The fluctuating number of councilmembers is due to the complexities of the transfer process. The minimum and maximum membership counts of eight to sixteen were set by Burke and the first council in 1957, with a target number of twelve.

The council is often referred to internally as the "twelve", despite the membership having rarely actually been twelve.

### Operations

Operations stands between the council and the rest of the organisation. It handles finances, inter-department communications, disciplinary actions, human resources, and all other administrative tasks.

### Dispatch

Dispatch handles active cases, assigning units and centralising information.

#### Field Agent Units

Field agents are usually formed into units of around four or five. Some units are temporary, handling one or two cases before being disbanded. Others take on a perennial nature, lasting longer than the agents that compose them, replaced over time like the Ship of Theseus. Units are usually formed by Dispatch, but they are sometimes formed by the agents themselves. Dispatch has come to trust agents' intuitions when it comes to team composition.

### Research and Development

Very little is known about the beyond, it seems to have a consciousness of its own, and appears to mockingly change its own nature whenever things begin to make sense. Nevertheless, it must be studied. In fact, its fluctuating nature makes it all the more important to continuously observe and measure changes.

#### Reconnaissance

A sort of "exchange programme" with the Deep Reconnaissance Division. Troops from the DRD spend some time at the PCTF, mostly with the R&D department, but also embedded in units out in the field, sharing their own knowledge and learning from the PCTF.
The PCTF also occasionally sends agents to the DRD, but it is not as common, due to the DRD's less research-oriented nature. The DRD facilities aren't well adapted to host exchanges, and their field operations often require more specialised training.

#### Armoury

Archaic and idiosyncratic, the armoury is nevertheless a reliable part of PCTF operations. Despite the odd vocabulary (*e.g:* "clips" instead of "magazines") and the dismissive attitude, the armourers' expertise and craftsmanship is second to none. The signature modified Becker & Wolfe G909 semi-automatic pistol is the symbol of the armoury's dedication and skill.
Just swallow your pride while they scoff at your lack of knowledge and berate you for treating your assigned gear so carelessly, and they'll provide you with fine-tuned custom armaments adapted to your particular needs.

#### Special Acquisitions

With Special Acquisitions' approval, some experimental creations and found artifacts can be provided to units. This branch maintains a database of vetted items, classified and documented, in order to best evaluate requests, and provide usage guidelines.
Special Acquisitions is the only branch with the authority to directly inflict disciplinary strikes without going through a complaint to operations and a formal hearing. This exception was deemed necessary to properly dissuade agents from misusing the volatile items entrusted to them.

### Depository

All items and artifacts seized in the field are brought to the depository for safekeeping.
Most items are made available to research and development once a general risk assessment has been completed and the items are deemed safe enough to leave the depository.

#### Quarantine

Items that have not yet been assessed are kept in quarantine.

#### Stronghold

The stronghold is where the most dangerous and unstable items are stored.

#### Detainment

The PCTF is not in the business of keeping prisoners, but some individuals cannot be safely reintroduced to the general public even with a complete brainwipe. Detainment is a necessary evil. All detainees are regularly evaluated for release. Some are eventually deemed safe to rejoin civilian life, others are recruited into the PCTF or allowed to join the sanctuary.

### Sanctuary

Despite being repeatedly shut down, the Cult of the Wayfarer kept reappearing spontaneously. Eventually the decision was made to contain the cult within the PCTF complex and the sanctuary was built to house it.

The cult is mostly safe, and their hallucinatory ramblings contain a mix of lies and truths. Agents are warned to keep any thoughts and opinions to themselves within the sanctuary.

Some agents choose to join the cult upon retirement. Some detainees ask to join the cult, often despite never being informed of its existence. The cult is alse allowed to recruit from the civilian population, but no more than two new members per month or one hundred members overall (excluding PCTF-provided members). The cult has never sought to renegociate these terms.

#### Cynosure

A sort of infirmary where the cult provides spiritual healing. People who spend time there report lingering nightmares for a few weeks after they leave, but typically also see a significant improvement to beyond-inflicted illnesses and occasionally even gain insights into unresolved issues and impossible cases.

## The Beyond

A seemingly conscious collection of alternate realities and paranormal phenomena, slowly but surely seeping into realspace.

It is unclear whether or not the beyond is a single entity, nor even if the various alternate realities and paranormal phenomena are related to each other. They are nevertheless collectively referred to as "the beyond". People who are sensitive to one aspect of the beyond tend to be sensitive to most if not all other aspects. Various elements of the beyond often interact with each other. While some paranormal phenomena don't fit in perfectly with other observations of the beyond, the vast majority do. Almost all elements of the beyond display behaviours that seem conscious of human observation, responding in similar manners to external intervention.
It is possible that these connections are just due to the nature of paranormal phenomena, but the connections are strong enough that it has become useful to consider the beyond as though it were a singular entity, at least when it comes to solving cases that involve the paranormal.

The exact shape and nature of the beyond is far from well understood, and is almost certainly fuzzy and fractal-like. Despite this, some broad patterns have been identified and named.
Patterns have general classifications:
- Filter: Modifications to the rules or shape of reality
- Persona: Embodied into people (individuals or groups)
- Labyrinth: A place that takes on the properties of a pattern

Note: The names given to the patterns don't necessarily represent their actual appearance, but rather their general behaviour. The names are usually informed by their behaviour, but also their appearance upon first observation.
For instance, the Masquerade's first appearance was as an actual masquerade party, but it has also been observed as a science conference and as a music festival. The main notable feature of the Masquerade pattern is a large and hectic social gathering where it becomes more and more difficult to remember people and conversations.
